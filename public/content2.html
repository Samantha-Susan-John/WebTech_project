Top 5 waterfalls to visit in India

By John Doe

1. Jog Falls, Karnataka

Best time to visit: November to January

This waterfall is very popular and is marked as one of the UNESCO ecological hot spots in the world. It stands as second highest plunge falls in India; its height is 830 feet.
The sight from this spot is breathtaking, attracting tourists heavily, making it through to the top 10 waterfalls in India list.

One other feature about Jog Falls is that it is un-tiered waterfall in Karnataka, India, the water flows directly to the ground.
The intensity of the fall heightens during the monsoons giving it a more beautiful view. The vegetations that grow around the falls are complimentary to it

2. Meenmutty Falls, Kerala

Best time to visit: September to June

Meenmutty means where fish gets blocked, it is located in Wayanad District in the state of Kerala, India. It is a three-tiered waterfall with a height of 300 meters.
The sight of the waterfalls is only visible after a hike of about 2 km through the jungle but is worth the walk.

Meenmutty stands as second largest waterfall in the state of Kerala, the water descends from nearly 1000 ft over three stages. If you are travelling uphill to Ooty, you can drop visit this fall as it lies on the main road to Ooty.

3. Langshiang Falls, Meghalaya

Best time to visit: October to December and March to May

Meenmutty means where fish gets blocked, it is located in Wayanad District in the state of Kerala, India. It is a three-tiered waterfall with a height of 300 meters.

4. Vantwang Falls, Mizoram

Best time to visit: September to January

This waterfall is located in Mizoram, about 137 km from Aizwal and about 5 km from the village of Thenzawl in Serchhip district. It is the highest waterfall in Mizoram (750 ft) and 13th highest in India.
Vantawng Falls is made by the Vanva River and the waterfall creates seven storied cliff while descending.

The name of the waterfall comes from a great swimmer, Vantawngs, it is said that he used to swim like a fish. It is surrounded by a lush green forest, giving it a scenic view.


5. Dudhsagar Falls, Goa

Best time to visit: September to January

This waterfall is found in the high peaks of Western Ghats, south of Goa and close to the Karnataka border. The name literally means “sea of milk”, and as the name suggests it does look like flowing sea of milk.

TThe height of this waterfall is 310 meters (1020 ft), 30 meters of width with four tires. Dudhsagar is at its peak flow during the monsoons, the sight becomes stunning.


6. 4 things you didn't know you could do in Andaman

Best time to visit: October to January

1. Go Bird Watching You may not know it, but Andaman is home to a huge flock of birds that can be seen and heard all around the island. Some of these birds are migratory, but many rare species can be spotted year-round! The interest in bird watching and photography has increased so much that tours have started being organised to spot them. The tours may take up the full day so plan accordingly. This local particular experience in Andaman can be thrilling for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. 2. Head On A Night Kayaking Trip Perhaps one of the most beautiful experiences in Andaman, night kayaking certainly deserves more hype. Now, we know that night kayaking can be done in several other places around the world but night kayaking through the bioluminescent effect of the water is truly just exclusive to Andaman. Explore the quiet of the islands on the calm backwaters of the Andaman Sea. Night Kayak around the region for a tour that takes you through the lush green mangroves of Havelock and introduces you to an experience that is both exciting and soothing at the same time. One of the safest and easiest water activities on the island, night kayaking is the best way to connect with water for non-swimmers. The scene is really mystical. This local experience in Andaman isn’t too tiring and can be experienced by anyone 3. Check Out The Mud Volcanoes Andaman is special and is coincidentally one of the few places in the world where you can witness mud volcanoes. These small muddy craters are created by natural gases emitted by decaying organic matter underground that push mud upwards. You shouldn’t expect something big on the lines of the typical volcano eruption or lava spurts, because none of that will really happen. Most of the time it’s just mud spurting out of the ground. However, it is a unique geological feature of Andaman that may be intriguing to those who research such phenomena. If possible, give this place a visit. 4. Sign Up For A Boat Trip To See Barren Island If you think Andaman can’t get any cooler, you’re in for a surprise. Despite being just 3 km in length, Barren Island is home to India’s largest active volcano! The volcano plays a key role in the annual temperature variation and keeping the continental climate stable. The island is uninhabited, and due to safety concerns, you will not be allowed to set foot on the island. The closest place to Barren Island is Havelock, from where you can take a ferry to see the island. A lot of great pictures can be taken of this volcanic island! While the normal attractions do steal the show most of the time, do make space in your schedules for some of these experiences if not all. They will undoubtedly prove to be worth your while.
